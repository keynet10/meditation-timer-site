How White Noise Works (The Acoustic Blanket)

Unlike music or nature sounds, true white noise contains every single frequency across the spectrum of human hearing, played at equal intensity. It sounds similar to a rushing waterfall, heavy television static, or a powerful fan.

Because it contains all frequencies simultaneously, it creates an "acoustic blanket" over your environment. If a dog barks down the street, that specific sound frequency is already playing within the white noise track, so your brain doesn't register the interruption. This phenomenon is known as auditory masking.

White noise isn't just for mindfulness. For millions of people suffering from tinnitus (a persistent ringing in the ears), absolute silence can be maddening.
